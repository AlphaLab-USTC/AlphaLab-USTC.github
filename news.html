Language Representations Can be What Recommenders Need: Findings and Potentials

2024 Leheng Sheng, An Zhang, Yi Zhang, Yuxin Chen, Xiang Wang, Tat-Seng Chua ICLR 2025 (Oral)

This work explores the potential of using language model representations for recommendation tasks. Surprisingly, we find that item representations linearly mapped from advanced LM representations yield superior recommendation performance, suggesting a possible homomorphism between language and recommendation spaces. We further develop a simple yet effective collaborative filtering model based on language representations, which outperforms leading ID-based models and demonstrates promising features like zero-shot recommendation abilities and user intention awareness.
